# Rival Hearts Rules And Balance Notes

## Current Design

Rival Hearts is now a simple two-player hidden-information game. The Suitor chooses the letter. The Confidant knows the hidden truth but cannot speak. The game keeps only the systems that create the strongest play:

- 3 routes: Fast, Safe, Secret.
- 3 tones: Tender, Bold, Honest.
- 4 signals: Heart, Thorn, Clock, Mask.
- 3 Confidant actions: Clear Danger, Delay Rival, Strengthen Letter.
- 1 hidden mood, 1 danger route, 1 Rival route.
- 1 Heart or 1 Rumour each round.

First to 3 Hearts wins. 3 Rumours loses.

## Round Rules

1. The Confidant sees the mood, danger route, and Rival route.
2. The Confidant places exactly two signals on routes or tones.
3. The Confidant chooses one secret action.
4. The Suitor chooses one route and one tone.
5. The hidden facts are revealed and one token is awarded.

## Resolution

| Situation | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Suitor chose the danger route and Clear Danger was not used | 1 Rumour |
| Suitor chose the Rival route, Delay Rival was not used, and the tone does not fit | 1 Rumour |
| Strengthen Letter was used | Tone counts as fitting the mood |
| Anything else | 1 Heart |

Signals do not directly score the round. They matter because they create shared interpretation under pressure.

## Balance Evaluation

The old version had strong theme, but too many systems were active at once. Cards, AP, obstacles, Rival traits, questions, tones, route math, and tiebreakers all competed for attention. Beginners had to learn the rules before they could enjoy the central idea.

The new version is smaller but deeper. Each mechanic has a clear job:

- Signals create ambiguity and communication.
- Routes create spatial risk.
- Tones keep romance central.
- The Rival creates hidden pressure.
- Actions let the Confidant protect one thing, not everything.

The key balance change is that the Confidant can only protect against one class of problem. Clear Danger does nothing against the Rival. Delay Rival does nothing against danger. Strengthen Letter helps only when tone is the issue. This creates meaningful tradeoffs with very low rules load.

Fun does not need to scale by adding more routes or more Rivals. It scales through combinations. With 3 routes, 3 tones, 4 signals, 3 actions, and overlapping hidden facts, the same rules can produce many different readings. A Mask on Secret can mean "Rival", "deception", or "do not trust the obvious path". A Heart on Bold can mean "this tone fits" or "I am pushing you toward confidence". The game changes because players change how they read each other.

## Why This Should Score Well

**Mechanics Design**: The design has novelty because it is a cooperative deduction game where one player has perfect information but only a tiny symbolic language. The decisions are meaningful because each signal and action changes what the Suitor can infer.

**Theme And Premise**: The court-romance theme and mechanics are tightly aligned. Social danger becomes hidden routes. Rivalry becomes a route threat. Court Silence becomes the central communication rule.

**Communication**: The rules can be taught quickly: two roles, three routes, three tones, four symbols, one action, one token. The resolution table makes outcomes clear and helps players improve their shared language.
